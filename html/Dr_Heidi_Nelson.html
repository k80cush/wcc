Dr. Heidi Nelson

Pioneering Surgeon and Oncological Researcher

"That's the stuff I enjoy: taking a problem and thinking, 'let's turn it around,'' and then doing it. I grew up believing that you should always try to make something better, to leave it a little better than you found it."

Dr. Heidi Nelson is a physician and researcher at Mayo Clinic in the field of colon and rectal cancer. The long-term goal of her research program is to improve both the duration of life and quality of life for patients with colon and rectal cancer. She has made use of both comprehensive clinical research databases and clinical trials to acheive her research goals. Two years ago Mayo named Nelson chair of surgery.

Interesting Facts

  1. Dr. Nelson was the first female surgeon at Mayo Clinic.
  2. It was clear to her that surgery was a perfect fit for her the first time she set foot in the operating room.
  3. Just three years after she was hired at Mayo she recieved a federal grant to study a new surgical technique called laparoscopy.
  4. Dr. Nelson planted more than 600 evergreen trees on her 21-acre property.
